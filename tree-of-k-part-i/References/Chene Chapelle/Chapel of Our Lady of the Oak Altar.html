Historical

This pilgrimage chapel was built between 1863 and 1869 according to the plans of the Lyon architect Pierre Bossan , at the instigation of Abbé Gros, parish priest of Scey-en-Varais , and Théodore Grosjean, chaplain of the Visitation convent in Ornans . It was intended to house the statuette of the Virgin and Child discovered nearby in the trunk of an oak tree in 1803. The chapel was consecrated on August 3, 1869.

The chapel is listed in the Mérimée database following the 1978 inventory [ 1 ] .

Legend

In early April 1803, a young girl reported seeing a white lady near an oak tree, known as the Our Lady Oak, who ascended to heaven in a bright light. On August 15 (the Feast of the Assumption ) of the same year, the girl, along with her father and sisters, saw a light emanating from this oak tree. The trunk of the tree was hollowed out, and a terracotta statue of the Virgin and Child holding a bunch of grapes was discovered . [ 2 ]

Geographical location

It is located in the Loue valley , on the right bank, on a plateau above a rise in terrain a few kilometers downstream from Ornans .

Description

It is a rectangular building, in the Romano-Gothic style, 23m long and 8.5m wide, ending in a rounded apse . The interior ceiling consists of groin vaults and pointed arch vaults .
